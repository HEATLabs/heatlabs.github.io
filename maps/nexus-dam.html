Nexus Dam

An industrial map featuring a massive dam structure, providing vertical gameplay opportunities and strategic choke points.

Select Gamemode

Map Introduction

Nexus Dam (Also known as Friendship Dam) was a “friendly collaboration” between one of two rivaling global powers and a developing country. Meant to provide electricity via modern power station to a brand-new town, the place is now a peaceful climate research centre amongst old caves that used to harbour insurgents – and just one good hit at a dam could start a catastrophe. There are plenty of high spots for long-range snipers as well as hiding places for heavier armored tanks to take those marksmen down. Dominate the high ground or crush still while rumbling through riverbed valleys and ramming snipers off the cliffs!

Terrain Features

Mostly rocky mountain terrain with a mix of a small town and village on one side with the dam and power plant occupying the other side.

Vision Control

Several elevated positions allow strong vision control over key pathways and objectives.

Map Flow

Open corridor style with three main paths providing ample cover for attacking/defending.

Standard Battle Overview

Your typical battle will have you attacking/defending the middle of the map around points B and C while protecting the point closest to your spawn (Spawn 1: Point E, Spawn 2: Point D). Point A happens to be one of several useful overwatch positions but is not regularly attacked.

Standard Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

These areas provide strategic advantages for controlling the flow of battle:

  • Point C Cliff: Dominated by hull-down tanks, useful for providing support to points B, D, and E
  • Town Square: Located beneath Point C Cliff, main brawling area with the ability to push into other objectives
  • Power Plant: Located on point A, has a large cliff with sightlines covering the majority of the map

Advanced Tactics

Early Game (Option 1)

  • Grab point D/E once the battle starts
  • Rush to a solid brawling position in point B
  • Be weary of flanking enemies

Early Game (Option 2)

  • Grab point D/E once the battle starts
  • Rush up the slope to point C and get into a hull-down position
  • Be weary of wide flanking enemies

Mid Game

  • After securing your base from early game, continuously rotate between points B, C, and D/E
  • Be weary of wide flankers who will attack from behind around point C
  • Push towards point D/E to start the "snowball"

Pro Tip: Standard Battle

Point A/Power plant is a point that many often forget about and has been the reason for loosing battles. It is best to keep an eye on it incase you notice an opening.

Encounter Battle Overview

In Encounter mode, the focus shifts to capturing a single central point while defending against enemy attacks from multiple directions.

Encounter Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

These areas are crucial for controlling the capture point:

  • Capture Zone: Heavily contested area requiring coordinated pushes
  • Northern Approach: Provides cover for medium tanks to support
  • Southern Flank: Fast route for light tanks to disrupt enemy

Advanced Tactics

Capture Strategy

  • Fast cap requires at least 3 tanks in circle
  • Staged approach with defenders on perimeter
  • Use smoke to conceal capping vehicles

Defense Strategy

  • Establish crossfire covering cap circle
  • Position TDs with clear lines of sight
  • Set up early warning system with scouts

Pro Tip: Encounter

Don't commit too many tanks to the capture point early in the match. It's better to secure the surrounding areas first and then move in to capture when you have established position control.

Assault Battle Overview

Assault mode transforms the map into an attacker/defender scenario, with one team attempting to capture the base while the other defends.

Assault Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

Different strategies for attackers and defenders:

Attackers

  • Eastern Push: Main assault route with cover
  • Western Flank: Secondary route to divide defenses
  • Artillery Support: Key for suppressing defenders

Defenders

  • Central Bunker: Strong defensive position
  • Flank Watchers: Prevent enemy flanking maneuvers
  • Fallback Positions: Secondary defensive lines

Advanced Tactics

Attacker Tactics

  • Coordinated push with multiple tank types
  • Use smoke shells to create advancing cover
  • Maintain pressure on all fronts to divide defense

Defender Tactics

  • Preset firing positions with overlapping fields
  • Flexible response force for breakthrough points
  • Conservative HP management for late-game defense

Pro Tip: Assault

As a defender, avoid the temptation to chase fleeing attackers. Maintaining your defensive line is more important than securing individual kills.

Tank-Specific Strategy

Different tank classes excel in various areas of this map. Here are specific recommendations for each tank type:

Defender

Recommended Areas:

  • Hull-down positions in point C
  • Town Square

  • Chopper - M1E1

  • Flanking area around point C

  • Raketa - T-62AV
  • Kent - HSTV-L
  • Reaper - Leopard 2k14

Marksman

  • Respective Spawn cliffs
  • Point A/Power plant cliff
  • CPoint C Cliff

  • Jager - Leopard 1A6A1 (Best sniper tank)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which spawn position has an advantage?

Based on personal experience, the north spawn (spawn 2) has a slight advantage in capturing point C due to the design of the hill. South Spawn (spawn 1) has the advantage in capturing point A due to pathing and cover. North spawn has a disadvantage as the nearby tunnel can be easily blocked. This disadvantage diminishes at higher skill levels as players know how to counter it.

How should I approach this map as a Marksman?

For Marksman, it is best to use your advantage in sniping. Some common positions are those with big sightlines behind the frontline.

Conclusion

This urban battleground offers diverse gameplay opportunities for all tank classes. Success relies on understanding the key positions, maintaining map control, and adapting to the flow of battle.

Whether you prefer the frontal brawling of heavy tanks, the flexible support of mediums, or the spotting tactics of light tanks, this map rewards thoughtful positioning and coordinated team play.
