Project Phoenix

A scientific research base set in the foothills of a mountain range offering a chaotic thunderdome with several flanking routes.

Select Gamemode

Map Introduction

This urban battlefield features a mix of narrow streets and open squares, creating diverse combat scenarios. Understanding the layout and key tactical positions is essential for victory in all game modes.

Terrain Features

Mix of urban structures, rubble piles, and open squares providing various engagement distances and cover options.

Vision Control

Several elevated positions allow strong vision control over key pathways and objectives.

Map Flow

Three main battle corridors with interconnecting side streets for tactical repositioning.

Standard Battle Overview

Standard battles on this map typically involve controlling three key areas while preventing enemy advances. The urban environment creates natural choke points and flanking routes.

Standard Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

These areas provide strategic advantages for controlling the flow of battle:

  • Central Square: Dominated by heavy tanks, provides access to both flanks
  • Eastern Alley: Quick route for light and medium tanks to flank
  • Western Buildings: Sniping positions overlooking key areas

Advanced Tactics

Early Game

  • Rush key spotting positions with light tanks
  • Establish defensive line in central area
  • Identify weaknesses in enemy deployment

Mid Game

  • Reinforce successful flanks
  • Retreat from overwhelmed positions
  • Control crossfire opportunities

Pro Tip: Standard Battle

The central square often becomes a deadlock early in the match. Consider sending a small force through the eastern alley to break the enemy's concentration and create opportunities for your main force to advance.

Encounter Battle Overview

In Encounter mode, the focus shifts to capturing a single central point while defending against enemy attacks from multiple directions.

Encounter Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

These areas are crucial for controlling the capture point:

  • Capture Zone: Heavily contested area requiring coordinated pushes
  • Northern Approach: Provides cover for medium tanks to support
  • Southern Flank: Fast route for light tanks to disrupt enemy

Advanced Tactics

Capture Strategy

  • Fast cap requires at least 3 tanks in circle
  • Staged approach with defenders on perimeter
  • Use smoke to conceal capping vehicles

Defense Strategy

  • Establish crossfire covering cap circle
  • Position TDs with clear lines of sight
  • Set up early warning system with scouts

Pro Tip: Encounter

Don't commit too many tanks to the capture point early in the match. It's better to secure the surrounding areas first and then move in to capture when you have established position control.

Assault Battle Overview

Assault mode transforms the map into an attacker/defender scenario, with one team attempting to capture the base while the other defends.

Assault Battle key positions and routes

Key Positions

Different strategies for attackers and defenders:

Attackers

  • Eastern Push: Main assault route with cover
  • Western Flank: Secondary route to divide defenses
  • Artillery Support: Key for suppressing defenders

Defenders

  • Central Bunker: Strong defensive position
  • Flank Watchers: Prevent enemy flanking maneuvers
  • Fallback Positions: Secondary defensive lines

Advanced Tactics

Attacker Tactics

  • Coordinated push with multiple tank types
  • Use smoke shells to create advancing cover
  • Maintain pressure on all fronts to divide defense

Defender Tactics

  • Preset firing positions with overlapping fields
  • Flexible response force for breakthrough points
  • Conservative HP management for late-game defense

Pro Tip: Assault

As a defender, avoid the temptation to chase fleeing attackers. Maintaining your defensive line is more important than securing individual kills.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which spawn position has the statistical advantage?

Based on win rate data, the north spawn has a slight advantage (51.8% vs 48.2%) primarily due to better access to the central high ground. This advantage diminishes at higher skill levels as players know how to counter it.

Is this map good for completing spotting missions?

This map offers moderate spotting opportunities, primarily in the eastern section and central crossroads. Light tanks can achieve 2,000-3,000 spotting damage in good matches, but it's not ideal for the highest-tier spotting missions.

What are the best artillery positions?

Artillery has limited effectiveness on this map due to the urban environment. The best positions are near the back corners of each base, focusing on the more open central area. Using a wider splash radius artillery piece is recommended.

How should I approach this map in a tank destroyer?

For turreted TDs, use the second-line positions behind your heavies. For fixed TDs, the western building area provides excellent firing lines with protection. Focus on controlling choke points rather than trying to cover the entire map.

Whether you prefer the frontal brawling of heavy tanks, the flexible support of mediums, or the spotting tactics of light tanks, this map rewards thoughtful positioning and coordinated team play.
