Aircraft Carrier

A tropical shipyard map centered around a grounded aircraft carrier, featuring dynamic elements like a moving barge and multiple flanking routes through naval warehouses.

Map Introduction

A cutting-edge super carrier, crippled by a “mysterious” collision during its Pacific transit, limps into a tiny tropical port for emergency repairs, and now rivaling powers scramble to seize its experimental tech. But neither side realizes a shadowy third party orchestrated the “accident,” and their trap is about to spring. Fight across a cluttered tropical shipyard dominated by the stranded carrier, and be aware of the moving barge. Capture its deck or flank through secret passages and a maze of naval warehouses. The ship hasn’t sailed yet but your chance to control it might.

Terrain Features

Mix of urban structures, rubble piles, and open squares providing various engagement distances and cover options.

Vision Control

Several elevated positions allow strong vision control over key pathways and objectives.

Map Flow

Three main battle corridors with interconnecting side streets for tactical repositioning.

Map Overview

Standard battles on this map typically involve controlling three key areas while preventing enemy advances. The urban environment creates natural choke points and flanking routes.

Pro Tip

The central square often becomes a deadlock early in the match. Consider sending a small force through the eastern alley to break the enemy's concentration and create opportunities for your main force to advance.

Class-Specific Strategy

Different tank classes excel in various areas of this map. Here are specific recommendations for each tank type:

Defender

Recommended Areas:

  • Central Square (hull-down positions)
  • Northern Corridor (sidescraping opportunities)
  • Base defense points

Best Performers:

  • T110E5 (excellent turret armor)
  • IS-7 (good mobility for city fighting)
  • Maus (ultimate blocking capability)

Assault

Recommended Areas:

  • Eastern Flank (mobility advantage)
  • Support positions behind heavy tanks
  • Central crossing (with caution)

Best Performers:

  • Object 140 (excellent DPM)
  • M48 Patton (view range advantage)
  • E 50 M (ram capability in urban fights)

Support

Recommended Areas:

  • Southern Passage (early spotting)
  • Central Crossing (mid-game)
  • Flanking routes around major engagements

Best Performers:

  • AMX 13 105 (clip potential)
  • T-100 LT (survivability in urban)
  • EBR 105 (for rapid repositioning)

Marksman

Recommended Areas:

  • Western Buildings (sniping positions)
  • Base defense (assault mode)
  • Choke point coverage

Best Performers:

  • Object 268 v4 (city brawling capability)
  • Strv 103B (excellent for fixed positions)
  • Grille 15 (mobility between positions)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which spawn position has the statistical advantage?

Based on win rate data, the north spawn has a slight advantage (51.8% vs 48.2%) primarily due to better access to the central high ground. This advantage diminishes at higher skill levels as players know how to counter it.

Is this map good for completing spotting missions?

This map offers moderate spotting opportunities, primarily in the eastern section and central crossroads. Light tanks can achieve 2,000-3,000 spotting damage in good matches, but it's not ideal for the highest-tier spotting missions.

What are the best artillery positions?

Artillery has limited effectiveness on this map due to the urban environment. The best positions are near the back corners of each base, focusing on the more open central area. Using a wider splash radius artillery piece is recommended.

How should I approach this map in a tank destroyer?

For turreted TDs, use the second-line positions behind your heavies. For fixed TDs, the western building area provides excellent firing lines with protection. Focus on controlling choke points rather than trying to cover the entire map.

Conclusion

This urban battleground offers diverse gameplay opportunities for all tank classes. Success relies on understanding the key positions, maintaining map control, and adapting to the flow of battle.

Whether you prefer the frontal brawling of heavy tanks, the flexible support of mediums, or the spotting tactics of light tanks, this map rewards thoughtful positioning and coordinated team play.
